FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Here are a few questions that our customers ask

What is a VIN
‚ñ∂

A Vehicle Identification Number (VIN) is a unique 17-character code assigned to every vehicle. It serves as the vehicle's fingerprint and contains information about the manufacturer, model, year, and other specifications.

Where do you find the VIN?
‚ñ∂

The VIN can be found in several locations: on the driver's side dashboard visible through the windshield, on the driver's side door frame, on the vehicle registration, insurance card, or title documents.

Where does the Vehicle History Report information come from?
‚ñ∂

Our vehicle history reports are compiled from multiple reliable sources including state DMVs, insurance companies, auto auctions, service centers, and other official databases to provide comprehensive vehicle information.

What types of vehicles can I run a Vehicle History Report on?
‚ñ∂

You can run vehicle history reports on cars, trucks, motorcycles, RVs, and other motor vehicles that have a valid 17-character VIN. This includes vehicles from 1981 to present.

Who operates and manage NMVTIS?
‚ñ∂

NMVTIS (National Motor Vehicle Title Information System) is operated by the U.S. Department of Justice and managed by the Bureau of Justice Assistance to help prevent fraud and protect consumers.
